Fog on Cadillac Mountain

Use the buttons below to view the painting larger or in an example picture frame:

Click to view:NormalLargerFrame 1Frame 2

Floating style Frame #2 is for paintings with image not cropped by rabbet.

Trying to paint with watercolors on Cadillac mountain could test the soul of any brush. The fog was so thick you could hear it dripping from the balsam fir trees onto the brittle lichens clinging to the pale pink granite. Perfect paper and pigment wash control was assured but one problem remained: frequent blinking and squinting wouldn't bring the subject into focus.
The solution? Forget the whole thing and bring the recollection back to the studio and let the slow drying time of the oils blend it all together. Try layer over layer of subtle grey glazes to see if some depth of field surrounding the evergreens could be developed. An unidentified thrush of some type kept gathering juniper berries and flying off into the vague primordial space with complete confidence.
